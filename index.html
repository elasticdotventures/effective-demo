CommerceRack: Executive Summary

CommerceRack enables retailers to move their business into the Cloud. Cloud computing offers businesses on-demand, and elastic scalability, while leveraging economies of scale to reduce costs. CommerceRack offers it is a cloud service that can facilitate both mobile and browser experiences both customers in store and at home, while also sending inventory to marketplaces like eBay, and Amazon.

High level functionality:

  • deliver /app based/ websites, with a shopping cart, and accept orders
  • automatically connect with marketplaces like with amazon, ebay, rakutan, newegg, sears and others.
  • host product catalog, inventory, and supply chain in the cloud
  • access order management, shipping, tracking, crm/wholesale, and warehouse data from any device, anywhere in the world, anytime.

Business is changing:

  • with mobile and social customers are connected all the time.
  • customers are buying more online every day, but are using marketplaces like amazon or ebay where fees are high and margins for retailers are razor thin.
  • the Cloud uses massive economies of scale to deliver business friendly services including dramatic cost savings and unparalleled functionality.

CommerceRack software is necessary for transactional commerce on a cloud.

About Us

CommerceRack and anyCommerce started out as concepts in 2009 by the developers of Zoovy, a privately held small business e-commerce website hosting and marketplace management company with 13 years of expertise in e-commerce. CommerceRack is a cloud service infrastructure that enables transactional commerce. CommerceRack utilizes anyCommerce's open-source framework to create unified experiences for e-commerce, m-commerce, social commerce and retail/brick and mortar businesses.

By 2011, most browsers on the market were releasing versions which could run the anyCommerce technology, and cloud services were rapidly growing in both popularity and stability. Recognizing the size and scope of this opportunity led to a decision by Zoovy's management team to spend six months focusing on a prototype which was completed in early 2012.

After numerous unforeseen delays and failed light-bulb experiments, the decision to stop boarding new clients and focus all energies toward developing exclusively for the cloud was made as it proved to be the best way to pursue the company's long term revenue goals. An end of life sunset date for SAAS hosted sites was announced and all resources were channelled into cloud products. The Zoovy system was migrated to the new anyCommerce technology and the CommerceRack project was unveiled in Q1 of 2014.

Build It and They Will Buy
(... and have a better experience!)

At the center of your e-commerce universe is the DataFair which stores information in the cloud for apps. DataFairs have several advantages over databases:

  • Improved Security: DataFairs use a single api + access control layer which is completely separated from the display logic. This results in a significantly reduced threat profile. Since there is a single point of access control, the session management system using role based permissions is able to determine the regular 'usage' for a role and flag exceptions. Future versions will have the capability to throttle/pause suspicious behavior then alert security administrators via SMS or App-push. For example when an admin user is accessing data records at a rate faster than their job role requires or on specially configured honey pot objects. Either which could indicate a compromised account with admin priviledges.
  • Faster Access Times: Intelligent Caching by the DataFair can determine frequently used data and make sure sstored in memory when requested. In addition hot objects can be synchronized and cached locally on apps, saving bandwidth, and i/o. This unique approach to caching significantly reduces costs in environments where these services are billable.
  • Faster Development: a single interface to many services and data means it's easy to get more done in less time.

Around the world designers, developers and testers are chomping at the bit to work on your apps. We have standards and guides for them to go by. Each application will start at the DESIGN stage, move to the DEVELOPMENT stage, and then proceed to the TESTING stage.
